A symmetric matrix need not be invertible; it could even be a matrix of zeros. _But if \(A^{-1}\) exists it is also symmetric_. From formula (ii) above, the transpose of \(A^{-1}\) always equals \((A^{\rm T})^{-1}\); for a symmetric matrix this is just \(A^{-1}\). \(A^{-1}\) equals its own transpose; it is symmetric whenever \(A\) is. Now we show that _multiplying any matrix \(R\) by \(R^{\rm T}\) gives a symmetric matrix_.
